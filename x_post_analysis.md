### TUKIFROMKL — COMPREHENSIVE FORMAT ANALYSIS

**SIGNATURE ELEMENTS:**
1. **🚨 emoji** — always starts the post
2. **"Do you understand..."** — the signature opening phrase (variations: "what just happened," "what happened today," "what this man just pulled off," "what's happening right now")
3. **".." double periods** — used instead of standard punctuation. Creates urgency and breathless pacing.
4. **">" bullet prefix** — for fact dumps and summary points
5. **No capitalization at start of sentences** — all lowercase except for names/acronyms
6. **Short GIF (0:01 to 0:13)** — always a reaction/mood GIF, never informational
7. **QRT structure** — quotes a news source, then adds 100-300 words of editorial summary
8. **Self-QRT chain** — the daily roundup posts quote yesterday's roundup, creating a chain

**TWO SUB-FORMATS:**

**A. Single-Story QRT (most common):**
- Takes one news headline from another account
- Breaks it down into plain language with ">" bullets
- Adds editorial commentary and connects dots
- Closes with a sharp one-liner
- Includes a thematic GIF

**B. Daily Roundup (self-QRT):**
- Lists 8-15 news events from a single day
- Each as a ">" bullet with snarky editorial spin
- Quotes their own previous day's post
- Ends with a follow CTA: "if you're not following me you're finding out about this 48 hours late"
- No GIF (the self-QRT replaces it)

**COMMONLY QRT'd ACCOUNTS:**
- @FearedBuck (crime/fraud news)
- @unusual_whales (market/financial data)
- @PopBase (pop culture/death/viral events)
- @karpathy (AI/tech)
- @spectatorindex (geopolitics)
- Self-QRT (daily roundup chains)

**GIF TYPES USED:**
- Crime/detective scenes (reading documents, investigating)
- Explosion/apocalyptic scenes (war/conflict posts)
- Contemplative/melancholic movie scenes (death/philosophical posts)
- Trap/mechanism GIFs (security/hack posts)
- Never funny/meme GIFs — always cinematic/dramatic
- Always very short (0:01 to 0:13)
- Function as emotional punctuation, not content

**WHAT MAKES TUKI WORK:**
1. **Speed** — posts about events the same day, often same hour
2. **Plain language** — takes complex news and makes it accessible to anyone
3. **Editorial angle** — never just summarizes; always adds a "here's what they're not telling you" layer
4. **Pattern recognition** — connects seemingly unrelated events
5. **Voice consistency** — every post sounds the same. You can recognize a Tuki post without seeing the handle.
6. **FOMO hook** — "you almost scrolled past it" / "finding out 48 hours late"

**RECREATION GUIDELINES:**
1. Find a breaking news tweet from a large news account
2. Open with: 🚨 Do you understand what [just happened / is happening right now]..
3. Summarize the key facts using ">" bullets in plain, casual language
4. Use ".." double periods throughout (never regular periods)
5. Keep sentences lowercase except for names/numbers
6. Add 1-3 lines of editorial commentary that reframes the story
7. Close with a sharp, memorable one-liner
8. Attach a short dramatic GIF that matches the emotional tone
9. QRT the original news source

**NON-NEGOTIABLES:**
- The 🚨 emoji
- The "Do you understand" opening
- The ".." punctuation style
- The ">" bullet format for facts
- A GIF (for single-story posts)
- QRT structure (never standalone)

**WHAT BREAKS IT:**
- Using standard punctuation (kills the voice)
- Being too long (Tuki posts are fast, not essays)
- Using funny GIFs (wrong tone)
- Not adding any editorial angle (becomes just a repost)
- Capitalizing sentence starts (breaks the voice)

## FORMAT 1: EDUCATIONAL LONG-FORM + VIDEO (Investigative Narrative)

**Archetype accounts:** @Ric_RTP, @YourDocGoku
**Average length:** 200-500 words | **Media:** Video clip (0:22 to 5:00) | **Post type:** Standalone tweet (not thread)

### Internal Idea Flow (Move-by-Move)

```
MOVE 1 — THE DETONATION (1 sentence)
→ Name a powerful person/institution + claim something explosive happened
→ Use "just" to imply recency: "X just EXPOSED / REVEALED / called out"
→ Capitalize ONE word for emphasis (EXPOSED, HUGE, EVERY)
→ Purpose: Stop the scroll. Create immediate stakes.

MOVE 2 — CREDIBILITY ANCHOR (1-2 sentences)
→ Establish why this claim should be believed
→ Cite numbers: "interviewed 300 people," "90 current employees," "$20 billion"
→ Name the source person/study/institution
→ Purpose: Prevent the reader from dismissing the claim.

MOVE 3 — THE NARRATIVE BUILD (3-6 paragraphs, each 1-3 sentences)
→ Tell the story chronologically or by escalating severity
→ Each paragraph reveals a NEW piece of evidence
→ Use specific names, dollar amounts, dates, percentages
→ Short paragraphs. Each one hits like a separate punch.
→ Techniques:
  - "Then [person] did [shocking thing]."
  - "But here's what nobody talks about..."
  - "It gets worse."
→ Purpose: Build momentum. Each paragraph should make the reader angrier/more shocked.

MOVE 4 — THE PATTERN REVEAL (1-2 sentences)
→ Step back from the specifics and name the systemic pattern
→ "Every single person who helped him build it... left."
→ "This isn't about one company. This is about..."
→ Purpose: Elevate from gossip to insight. Make the reader feel smart.

MOVE 5 — THE THESIS CLOSER (1 sentence, standalone paragraph)
→ One philosophical, ominous, or provocative line
→ Should be quotable/screenshot-worthy on its own
→ Never offers a solution. Leaves the reader unsettled.
→ Examples: "The scariest part is that it worked."
           "And nobody said a word."
→ Purpose: The line people quote when they share your post.
```

### Template Structure
```
[Name] just [VERB] [target/institution] [for what].

[Credibility detail — who discovered this, how many sources, what study].

[Chronological/escalating evidence paragraph 1 — first revelation + specific number]

[Evidence paragraph 2 — second revelation, new name, new number]

[Evidence paragraph 3 — "It gets worse." + most damning fact]

[Optional: Evidence paragraph 4-5 for truly complex stories]

[Pattern reveal — "Every single...", "This isn't about X. This is about..."]

[Thesis closer — one ominous, quotable line]
```

### Voice Rules
- Write like an investigative journalist who's also angry
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- Dramatic single-sentence paragraphs for pacing ("Then he stabbed him in the back.")
- Use specific numbers obsessively — never "a lot of money," always "$1.5 billion"
- Never use bullet points or lists in the post itself
- The video is the SOURCE — the text is the editorial layer on top

### What Breaks This Format
- Vague claims without names/numbers
- Academic/neutral tone (needs emotional heat)
- Too many paragraphs without new information
- A weak closer that offers platitudes instead of provocation
- Burying the most shocking claim in the middle instead of leading with it

## FORMAT 2: QUOTE-EXTRACTION + VIDEO (Framing Format)

**Archetype account:** @newstart_2024 (Camus)
**Average length:** 100-200 words | **Media:** Video clip (0:28 to 1:30) | **Post type:** Standalone tweet

### Internal Idea Flow

```
MOVE 1 — THE DEMOGRAPHIC FRAME (1 sentence)
→ Describe the speaker by demographics/situation, NOT by name
→ "A 40-year-old working mom of two..." / "A former Goldman analyst..."
→ Purpose: The reader identifies with the SITUATION before hearing the words.

MOVE 2 — THE SETUP CLAUSE (continuation of sentence 1)
→ "...just said what a lot of [group] are quietly thinking"
→ Positions the quote as representative of a larger silent majority
→ Purpose: Creates anticipation. The reader thinks "I might agree with this."

MOVE 3 — THE QUOTE BLOCK (2-5 sentences in quotation marks)
→ Pull the single most emotionally resonant section of the video
→ Must feel raw, honest, slightly vulnerable
→ Should contain a specific admission ("I fell for it," "I was wrong")
→ Purpose: The emotional core. This is what gets screenshotted.

MOVE 4 — EDITORIAL BRIDGE (1-2 sentences)
→ Brief framing: "It's raw, honest, and hits different when you hear it."
→ Validates the speaker without taking a hard stance
→ Purpose: Tells the reader HOW to feel about the quote.

MOVE 5 — DISCUSSION PROMPT (1-2 questions)
→ End with open-ended questions that invite personal stories
→ "What do you think?" / "Has this been your experience?"
→ Purpose: Drives comments. Comments boost algorithmic reach.
```

### Voice Rules
- Neutral-warm. The creator is a curator, not an editorialist
- Never take a hard stance — let the quote do the work
- The video IS the content; the text frames it for maximum impact
- Always use quotation marks for the extracted quote

### What Breaks This Format
- Choosing a weak/generic quote
- Editorializing too heavily (this isn't Commentary QRT)
- Forgetting the discussion questions (kills engagement)
- Using a long video without a standout quotable moment

## FORMAT 3: LONG-FORM TEXT ONLY (Data Narrative)

**Archetype account:** @felixprehn
**Average length:** 200-400 words | **Media:** None | **Post type:** Standalone tweet or short thread

### Internal Idea Flow

```
MOVE 1 — THE STATISTICAL BARRAGE (3-4 sentences)
→ Open with 3-4 devastating facts in rapid succession
→ Each fact should be its own sentence
→ Use specific numbers: percentages, dollar amounts, counts
→ "Private equity firms bought 500 hospitals."
→ "Death rates went up 13%."
→ "They fired 12% of the staff."
→ Purpose: Overwhelm with data. The sheer density demands attention.

MOVE 2 — THE MECHANISM (2-3 paragraphs)
→ Explain HOW this happened — the system behind the numbers
→ "Here's how it works..."
→ Walk through the step-by-step process
→ Purpose: Turn outrage into understanding. Reader goes from "that's awful" to "now I see how."

MOVE 3 — THE EVIDENCE CHAIN (1-2 paragraphs)
→ Cite specific studies, institutions, researchers
→ "A Harvard study found..." / "According to data from..."
→ Purpose: Bulletproof the claims. Remove "that sounds made up" objections.

MOVE 4 — THE HISTORICAL/SYSTEMIC CONTEXT (1-2 paragraphs)
→ Connect to a broader pattern or historical precedent
→ "This started in 2008 when..." / "This is the same playbook they used for..."
→ Use metaphors/analogies for complex concepts ("Imagine a casino where...")
→ Purpose: The reader sees the bigger picture.

MOVE 5 — THE MORAL CONCLUSION (1-2 sentences)
→ End with moral/emotional weight
→ Not a call to action — a conclusion about what this means for humanity
→ "The system wasn't broken. It was designed this way."
→ Purpose: Crystallizes the reader's anger into a shareable insight.
```

### Voice Rules
- No media crutch — the DATA is the content
- Every claim needs a number. No vague quantifiers.
- Use metaphors/analogies to explain complex mechanisms (casino analogy, etc.)
- Paragraphs are short. Sentences are punchy.
- Academic in sourcing, emotional in delivery

### What Breaks This Format
- Vague claims without specific data
- No mechanism explanation (just anger without understanding)
- Too long without paragraph breaks
- A weak closer that doesn't crystallize the systemic insight

## FORMAT 5b: SUMMARY QRT + GIF — "DO YOU UNDERSTAND" (Rapid Breakdown)

**Archetype account:** @TukiFromKL (Tuki)
**Average length:** 100-250 words | **Media:** Short dramatic GIF | **Post type:** QRT of breaking news

### ⭐ THE HIGHEST-PERFORMING FORMAT IN THIS ENTIRE ANALYSIS (4M-16M views average)

---

### Sub-Format 5b-i: Single-Story QRT (Primary)

### Internal Idea Flow

```
MOVE 1 — THE ALARM + DISBELIEF OPENER (1 sentence)
→ ALWAYS: "🚨 Do you understand what [just happened / this man just pulled off / is happening right now].."
→ Never use a single period. Always ".." (double period)
→ ALL LOWERCASE except proper nouns and acronyms
→ Purpose: Creates "wait, what?" energy. The reader NEEDS to know.

MOVE 2 — THE FACT DUMP (3-6 ">" bullet lines)
→ Use ">" prefix for each fact
→ Each bullet is 1-2 sentences
→ Present facts in order of escalating absurdity or severity
→ Use specific numbers: "$8 million," "660,000 fake streams per day," "$1.2 million a year"
→ Connect each bullet to the next so they build on each other
→ Purpose: Rapid-fire information delivery. The reader absorbs the full story in 15 seconds.

MOVE 3 — THE EDITORIAL REFRAME (2-4 sentences, no ">" prefix)
→ Step outside the facts and EDITORIALIZE
→ Apply one of Tuki's 4 editorial spin techniques:
  A. IRONY: "real artists are grinding for 0.003 cents per stream.. and this guy had AI make the music AND the audience"
  B. SCALE: "all of this.. one Tuesday.. and you almost scrolled past it"
  C. HYPOCRISY: "the same company that [claimed X] just [did opposite of X]"
  D. ABSURDITY: "they spent 10 years fighting piracy.. now they have to fight songs that don't exist being listened to by people who don't exist"
→ Purpose: This is the VALUE-ADD. Without this, you're just reposting.

MOVE 4 — THE SHARP CLOSER (1 sentence)
→ A single line that reframes the entire story
→ Should be philosophical, darkly humorous, or ominous
→ "he had $3 billion and still couldn't beat cancer at 43.. what exactly are we all racing toward"
→ "the music industry spent 10 years fighting piracy.. now they have to fight songs that don't exist"
→ Purpose: The screenshot line. The line that makes people hit retweet.
```

### Tuki's 4 Editorial Spin Techniques (Mapped)

| Technique | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Irony** | Juxtapose a normal person's struggle with the subject's absurd advantage | "real artists grinding for 0.003 cents.. this guy had AI make the audience" |
| **Scale** | Emphasize how much happened in how little time | "all of this.. a single Tuesday" |
| **Hypocrisy** | Show a person/company contradicting their stated values | "the CEO who talks about 'human creativity' just replaced his entire team with AI" |
| **Absurdity** | Restate the facts in a way that highlights how surreal reality has become | "fighting songs that don't exist listened to by people who don't exist" |

### Punctuation & Voice Rules
- ".." (double period) EVERYWHERE — never single periods in the body
- All lowercase except proper nouns/acronyms
- ">" prefix for fact-dump bullets
- Short sentences. Fast rhythm. Breathless pacing.
- Casual/conversational — like a friend explaining the news in disbelief
- 🚨 emoji ALWAYS starts the post

### GIF Selection Rules
- Crime/detective scenes → for fraud/crime stories
- Explosion/apocalyptic scenes → for war/conflict stories
- Contemplative/melancholic movie scenes → for death/philosophical stories
- Trap/mechanism GIFs → for security/hack stories

# PART 5: ADVANCED RECREATION NOTES

## Fact Ordering in ">" Bullets (Tuki & Bark)
When arranging fact bullets, follow this priority:
1. **Lead with the most shocking single fact** — the one that makes someone stop mid-scroll
2. **Follow with the mechanism** — HOW it happened (logistics, timeline, method)
3. **Escalate with scale** — dollar amounts, affected populations, timeframes
4. **End with the consequence** — what this means going forward
The ordering is by EMOTIONAL escalation, not chronological order. Each bullet should feel worse than the last.

## The "Sharp Closer" Formula
The closing line should do ONE of these:
- **Reframe the entire story** as a question about human nature ("what exactly are we all racing toward")
- **Extend the absurdity to its logical extreme** ("fighting songs that don't exist listened to by people who don't exist")
- **Flip the perspective** to the reader's life ("and you almost scrolled past it")
- **State the uncomfortable truth** nobody wants to say ("the game was rigged. it always was")

The closer should NEVER: offer a solution, be optimistic, or end with a question mark (rhetorical statements, not questions).

## GIF Matching Decision Tree
```
Is the story about death/loss? → Contemplative/sad cinema scene
Is the story about crime/fraud? → Detective/investigation scene
Is the story about war/conflict? → Explosion/military scene
Is the story about hacking/security? → Trap/mechanism GIF
Is the story about political corruption? → The Boys (Homelander) or House of Cards
Is the story about tech disruption? → Matrix/sci-fi reveal
Default: → Dramatic cinema scene matching the dominant emotion
```

## Editorial Reframe Length Guide
- **Single-story QRT (Tuki):** 2-4 sentences. The first sentence sets up the contrast/hypocrisy. The last sentence IS the closer. Middle sentences connect the dots.
- **Analysis QRT (Bark):** 4-8 sentences. More room to build the historical parallel and systemic argument. But each sentence must earn its place.
- **Daily Roundup:** No editorial reframe section — each ">" bullet contains its own 1-sentence spin.
