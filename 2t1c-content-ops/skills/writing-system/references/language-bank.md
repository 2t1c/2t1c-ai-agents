# Language Bank Reference

## The 5 Emotional Triggers

**1. Fear of Being Left Behind**
Reader suspects the world is moving and they're not.
Key words: quietly, nobody's talking about, before anyone else, most overlooked, silently, while everyone's focused on

**2. Curiosity Gap**
Brain cannot tolerate an open loop.
Key words: what he did next, the real reason, what really happened, you'll never see X the same, the part nobody tells you

**3. Institutional Betrayal**
Validates latent distrust toward authorities.
Key words: won't tell you, fought to keep hidden, exposed, finally, secret, the truth behind, kept it hidden for X years

**4. Awe at Scale**
Subject defied the laws of reality. Numbers are the weapon.
Key words: greatest, insane, mind-blowing, impossible, [number] in [short timeframe], the largest in history

**5. Identity Mirror**
The story is a reflection of the reader's own life or fear.
Key words: your, every X must, what you need to know, once you understand, how you should prepare

---

## Strongest Emotion Pairings

- Fear of Being Left Behind + Identity Mirror → most urgent
- Curiosity Gap + Awe at Scale → most addictive
- Institutional Betrayal + Identity Mirror → most anger-driven
- Awe at Scale + Curiosity Gap → natural for business/historical hooks
- Fear of Being Left Behind + Institutional Betrayal → natural for geopolitical hooks

## Credibility Rules

**Numbers must be exact and comparative:**
- Not "billions" → "$7.4 BILLION"
- Not "quickly" → "in 28 days," "in 90 days," "in 6 months"
- Not "a lot of debt" → "$990 MILLION of debt"
- Not "many soldiers" → "11 million soldiers"

**Comparisons must be specific and unexpected:**
- Not "very poor" → "poorer than Chad, Bangladesh, and Malawi"
- Not "much bigger" → "3x bigger than New York City"
- Not "enormous" → "a train station the size of 88 football fields"

**Parenthetical anchors for large numbers:**
- "worth more than $30 trillion (the entire US economy)"
- "$155 (a tiny fraction of the United States' $10,000+)"

**Speed reveals always use "just":**
- "collapsed in just 28 days" — not "collapsed in 28 days"
- The word "just" makes speed feel violent, not merely factual

**Adversarial verbs always active and named:**
- Never: "Brazil's rubber dominance eventually ended"
- Always: "The British sent a spy to destroy it"
- The adversary's action must be deliberate, not accidental

## Fixed Phrases Bank (Transplant Verbatim)

These are proven constructions. Transplant them directly — swap only the subject.

**FOMO / Hidden knowledge:**
- "Nobody is talking about..."
- "Here's what the media isn't telling you:"
- "While everyone's focused on X, Y..."
- "...quietly becoming..."
- "The story nobody tells about..."

**Delivery promises:**
- "Here's the forgotten story everyone should know:"
- "Here's the story they never taught you in school:"
- "Here's what you need to know and how you should prepare:"
- "Let me save you [X] hours — here are the [N] most important things:"

**Contrast constructions:**
- "If you think [X] was impressive, this would blow your mind:"
- "Not in X. Not in Y. Not anywhere."
- "Not with missiles. Not with troops. Not with nuclear weapons."

**Suppressed truth:**
- "For [N] years, [institution] fought to keep this hidden."
- "Now we finally know why."
- "These documents reveal what really happened — and it wasn't [official story]."

**Underdog validation:**
- "His competitors laughed."
- "Everyone in [institution] thought it was doomed."
- "Steve was right. Everyone was wrong."

**Scale injectors:**
- "The largest [X] in history"
- "No [entity] in history has ever [done X]"
- "This is history's most [adjective] [noun]:"

## Narrator Voice Phrases

Not locked to any position. Available at any point — first line, mid-thread, closer.
Any phrase from this bank can serve as a hook opener.
Use when the content earns it. Falls flat when decorating something ordinary.

**Job 1 — Pacing Reset** (after dense information sections)
- Stay with me on this one...
- Bear with me — this gets wild...
- Read every line of this carefully...
- Don't skip ahead on this one...
- Give me 60 seconds and I'll change how you see this forever...
- This one is worth your full attention...
- Clear your head before reading this...

**Job 2 — Anticipation Builder** (before a major reveal)
- This is where the story completely changes...
- This is the detail that changes everything...
- This is the part nobody tells you...
- This is where it stops being a history lesson...
- And then it gets even better...
- I promise this is going somewhere unexpected...

**Job 3 — Credibility Through Vulnerability** (admit uncertainty or surprise)
- I genuinely don't know how this ends...
- This kept me up at night...
- I went down a rabbit hole on this and never came back...
- I've spent weeks trying to make sense of this...
- This completely changed how I think about...
- Honestly this humbled me more than I expected...

**Job 4 — Permission to Feel** (immediately after a surprising fact lands)
- I swear this has to be...
- This broke my brain when I first found it...
- This shouldn't be possible. But it happened.
- I've read this a dozen times and it still doesn't feel real...
- No one talks about this and I genuinely don't understand why...
- I almost didn't believe this when I first read it...

**Job 5 — Stakes Elevation** (thread closers or before the final insight)
- This matters more than most people realize...
- The world looks different after you understand this...
- File this one away. It's going to matter...
- The surface story is interesting. What's underneath it is everything...
- The story everyone knows is only half of it...
- Most people will scroll past this...

## Formatting Rules (Non-Negotiable)

- One sentence per line
- No em dashes — split into new sentences instead
- Short sentences hit harder when they stand alone

**Character targets by format:**

**Threads — 250 to 300 characters total.**
The entire hook must be visible on the timeline without truncation. This is the hard target. Under 250 feels thin. Over 300 gets cut off. The structure that hits this range: one sharp opening (60-80 chars), one or two middle lines (100-120 chars), one closer (60-80 chars). Count before presenting.

**Long-form single posts — flexible, no hard ceiling.**
The scroll-stop moment must still land within the first 150 characters of the hook. After that, the hook can build as long as it needs to. 400-600 characters is a natural range. The compression rule does not apply — length is earned by the idea.

- The closer is not low-leverage — use the best possible closer, not the shortest
- Always output total character count in the certainty map so compliance is auditable

## The Contrast Construction Principle

[What everyone assumes] + [The reality is the opposite] + [One specific vivid detail that proves it]

Applied as a lens inside the template — not a replacement for it.
The contrast creates the curiosity gap. The vivid detail makes it undeniable.

---

## The Prioritization Principle

Before writing: "What is the ONE thing readers would feel most cheated for not knowing?"

Not the most impressive fact. Not the most dramatic.
The one that creates the biggest gap between what the reader assumed was true and what actually happened.

Lead with the gap, not the achievement.
