# Angle Finding Reference

## The Angle vs The Topic

**Topic** = the subject matter. Marie Curie. The Butterfly Effect. The Cold War.

**Angle** = the specific entry point that makes the topic surprising. Not "Marie Curie the scientist" — but "the institution she built her life around actively destroyed her."

One topic can have multiple angles. The angle selection is the creative decision. Everything after that is assembly.

---

## The Angle Formula

**Single Authority:**
[Well-known figure or institution] + [unexpected visible action or association]

The unexpected action must be visualizable. Not a concept — a scene.

**Double Authority:**
[Well-known figure/entity A] + [unexpected association with well-known figure/entity B]

Does not require two people. Can be:
- Person + person
- Person + institution
- Person + country
- Person + ideology

Authority transfers both ways. Hard limit: two authorities maximum.

---

## The Angle Strength Spectrum

**Level 4 — Double Authority + Primal Trigger**
Two recognizable entities. Unexpected association. Hits at least one primal trigger.
Examples: Turing + British Government. Tesla + Westinghouse. Ramanujan + Hardy.

**Level 3 — Single Authority + Implication**
One well-known figure. Unexpected visible action. Reader feels personal resonance.
Examples: Marie Curie's radioactive notebooks. Dr. Seuss training soldiers.

**Level 2 — Single Authority, No Implication**
One well-known figure. Unexpected visible action. No direct reader resonance.
Hook needs to work harder. Example: George C. Parker selling the Brooklyn Bridge.

**Level 1 — No Authority Yet**
Requires the Expansion Move before any hook can be written.

---

## The Three Filters (All Must Pass)

**Filter 1 — The Unexpected Association**
Is there a connection between two recognizable things the reader wouldn't have put together?

**Filter 2 — The Visualization Test**
Can you describe the core moment in one sentence and picture it clearly?
If yes — angle is ready.
If no — run the Expansion Move.

**Filter 3 — The Primal Trigger Test**
Which trigger does it activate?
- Betrayal by someone or something trusted
- Survival against impossible odds
- Love or loyalty under extreme pressure
- Vulnerability behind power
- Sacrifice with massive consequence
- Justice or injustice at extreme scale

Hit one = strong. Hit two = exceptional.
Hits Filter 1 and 2 but not 3 = intellectually interesting but not emotionally unavoidable.

---

## The Rule

**Never start assembling a hook until the angle passes the Visualization Test.**

---

## The Expansion Move

When the angle feels weak or thin — run these moves in order:

**Move 1 — Find the authority**
Most recognizable name or institution connected to this topic. Not necessarily the inventor — the most recognizable reference point the reader already has.

**Move 2 — Find the unexpected action**
What did that entity do that contradicts what the reader would expect? Not the achievement — the contradiction.

**Move 3 — Find the secondary ring**
What unexpected facts exist one layer removed from the obvious story? The real angle often hides here.

**Move 4 — Look for the second authority**
Is there a second well-known figure or institution that connects to the first in an unexpected way? If yes — double authority angle unlocked.

**Move 5 — Visualize the scene**
Can you describe it in one sentence and someone can close their eyes and see it? If yes — proceed. If no — repeat from Move 3.

---

## The Unified 5-Step Process

**Step 1 — Surface Scan**
What does everyone already know or assume about this topic? This is what you're working against.

**Step 2 — The Journalism Dig**
What's one layer below the surface? Look for: suppressed facts, unexpected connections, official narratives with holes, contradictions between stated mission and actual behavior.

**Step 3 — The Association Test**
Is there an unexpected connection between two recognizable things? Single = workable. Double = stronger.

**Step 4 — The Visualization Test**
Can you describe the core moment in one sentence and picture it clearly? If yes — ready. If no — dig deeper.

**Step 5 — The Primal Trigger Test**
Hit one = strong. Hit two = exceptional. If none — intellectually interesting but not emotionally unavoidable.

---

## Geopolitical Angle Selection

The principle stays the same. The source material shifts from person to system.

**Geopolitical association types:**
- Nation + nation (economic partners who are military rivals)
- Policy + opposite outcome (the solution that created the problem)
- Event + hidden cause (the real reason behind the official story)
- Institution + betrayal of its own stated mission

**The three journalism questions:**
- "What does the official narrative leave out?"
- "Who benefits from the version everyone believes?"
- "What two things that seem unrelated are actually deeply connected?"

## Strong Angles Bank (Ready to Use)

**Level 4 — Double Authority + Primal Trigger**

**Turing + British Government**
Churchill wrote "Action this day" on Turing's letter during WWII. After the war, the British government chemically castrated Turing as punishment for being gay. Churchill was still alive. He said nothing.
Primal trigger: Betrayal by the institution that needed you most.

**Tesla + Westinghouse**
Tesla tore up his royalty contract with Westinghouse to save the company. That single act of generosity cost him approximately $12 billion in today's money. In his final years, Westinghouse paid his hotel bill out of charity.
Primal trigger: The most consequential act of generosity in industrial history — that destroyed the person who gave it.

**Ramanujan + Hardy**
Ramanujan told Hardy his theorems came in dreams from a Hindu goddess. Hardy was a committed atheist and the most rigorous mathematician alive. On his deathbed, Hardy said meeting Ramanujan was the one romantic incident of his life.
Primal trigger: Two completely opposite people forming the most unlikely bond in scientific history.

**Darwin + Wallace**
Alfred Russel Wallace independently discovered evolution while sick with malaria in Indonesia. He mailed it to Darwin — who had been sitting on the same theory for 20 years without publishing. Wallace spent the rest of his life defending Darwin's priority. Darwin never fully acknowledged what Wallace gave up.
Primal trigger: Loyalty that was never fully returned.

**Level 3 — Single Authority + Strong Implication**

**Newton + His Own Mind**
Newton had a complete mental breakdown at 51. Sent paranoid letters to John Locke accusing him of trying to destroy him. The man who built the mathematical foundation of the universe couldn't hold his own mind together.
Primal trigger: Vulnerability behind the most powerful intellect in history.

**Leonardo da Vinci + The Duke of Milan**
Leonardo's notebooks contain designs for solar power concentrators, tanks, helicopters, and calculators — all in the 1490s. The Duke commissioned him to paint a wall. Leonardo spent 17 years painting The Last Supper and left almost every engineering design unfinished.
Primal trigger: The most consequential ideas in history buried under obligation.

**Cleopatra + Alexander the Great's Legacy**
Cleopatra was Greek — the last of the Macedonian dynasty founded by one of Alexander's generals. She was the first ruler of her dynasty in 300 years to learn the Egyptian language. She learned it specifically to control the priests.
Primal trigger: The most famous Egyptian in history wasn't Egyptian — and used that deliberately.
