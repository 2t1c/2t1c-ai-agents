# Thread Types Reference

## Important Note on Examples
The canonical examples below are a baseline — not an exhaustive list.
Always scan the full swipe file database for additional examples relevant to the topic.
A hook from a different thread type may contain a phrase or structure that fits better.
The goal is to use the full swipe file as a living reference bank.

On crossover: Always lead with one dominant type. Serving two equally produces diluted hooks.

---

## Thread Type 1 — Concept/Idea

**Dominant logic:** The concept is the hero. Everything serves to define, prove, and expand it.

**Distinct characteristics:**
- Concept either named upfront or revealed at the end after examples build to it
- Definition line mandatory — one sentence that makes the concept feel dangerous or high-stakes
- Examples illustrate the concept — they don't tell their own story
- KEY RULE: Stay 90%+ close to the original structure. Swap the subject, keep phrasing as close to verbatim as possible.

**What makes it credible:**
- Named authorities who believe in or discovered the concept
- Real documented examples that prove it
- The definition itself reframing something familiar as threatening

**Pre-built structures:**

Structure 1 — Concept Named At End:
Stack 2-3 vivid real examples → name the concept in ALL CAPS → plain closer.
"World War 1 could've been avoided if a car didn't take a wrong turn.
John F. Kennedy would've survived assassination if he didn't play a sport in childhood.
It all happened because of THE BUTTERFLY EFFECT.
Let's understand what that is:"

Structure 2 — Obsession Declaration:
Personal obsession opener → one dangerous definition → numbered closer.
"I'm obsessed with cognitive biases.
A 'cognitive bias' is a systematic error in thinking that destroys decision-making.
11 most powerful (and dangerous) cognitive biases I've found:"

Structure 3 — Authority Stack:
Name high-status believer → stack more authorities → triple benefit closer.
"Elon Musk is a big believer in Inversion Thinking.
Not just Musk — Albert Einstein, Charlie Munger, and Marcus Aurelius too.
Here's what it is, how to use it, and become better at it:"

Structure 4 — Origin Story:
Year anchor + named creator → name concept with dash reveal → paradox line → plain closer.
"In the 1960s, MIT Professor Jay Forrester created something that changed the way we think about supply chains — The Beer Game.
This game is a simple simulation, but it messed with the minds of thousands.
Here's the game:"

## Thread Type 2 — Historical/Geopolitical

**Dominant logic:** The system is the hero. Person only appears as agent of transformation.

**Distinct characteristics:**
- Always includes a specific year anchor
- Contrast between then and now must feel offensive or impossible
- Speed of change always specified with exact timeframe + "just"
- Named adversary always active, never passive
- Scale comparisons need parenthetical anchors for large numbers

**What makes it credible:**
- Exact numbers with unexpected comparisons
- Named institutions whose behavior proves the weight of the story
- Physical details that make abstract scale feel real

**Pre-built structures:**

Collapse Sequence: system at peak → single exposing event → speed of collapse + "just" → plain closer.
"They called Tunisia 'the perfect dictatorship.'
Ben Ali controlled everything: media, police, economy.
Then WikiLeaks exposed his family's $13B fortune.
His entire regime collapsed in just 28 days.
Here's how it happened:"

Transformation Arc: low point with offensive comparison → contrast jump to present → method closer.
"In 1978, China was poorer than Chad, Bangladesh, and Malawi.
It had a GDP per capita of $155.
But then Deng Xiaoping took power.
45 years later, China is a $17 Trillion world superpower.
Here's how Deng did it:"

Suppressed Truth: recency signal or fact → institution that fought to hide it → "finally" reveal → contrarian closer.
"80,000 secret JFK assassination files just dropped.
For 60 years, the CIA fought to keep them hidden. Now we finally know why.
These documents reveal what really happened in Dallas — and it wasn't a 'lone gunman.'"

Hidden Mechanic: system everyone knows → reframe what made it work → impossible detail → closer.
"Before global trade, there was the Silk Road.
A 4,000-mile route with no trucks, no internet — just one brilliant strategy.
Here's the strategy that kept this ancient trade network running smoothly:"

## Thread Type 3 — Biographical

**Dominant logic:** The person is the hero. The paradox is always the hook — not the achievement.

**Distinct characteristics:**
- Opens with highest claim or most extreme starting condition
- Named rivals or dismissers always present to create contrast
- Moral or legal stakes injected to create personal risk
- Achievement means nothing without the cost or contradiction attached

**What makes it credible:**
- Specific numbers tied to their decisions
- Named entities they fought against or were validated by
- The single most undeniable fact about their life most people don't know

**Pre-built structures:**

Superlative Setup: highest claim → negation staircase → paradox reveal → delivery closer.
"No one made more money in trading than Jim Simons.
Not Buffett. Not Soros. Not Dalio.
His hedge fund was so powerful, he shut it down to outsiders.
$100 in 1988 grew to $400M in 30 years.
Here's how a mathematician became the world's greatest trader:"

Countdown: ticking clock → stacked micro-facts → pivotal move → stakes injector → closer.
"In 2008, Elon pulled off the greatest comeback in corporate history.
Tesla was burning $4M per month on a car no one wanted. 0 sold. 3 weeks from bankruptcy.
Then he made a move so risky it almost landed him in prison.
Here's the crazy story:"

Villain/Hero Arc: disadvantage named → giant named → outcome revealed → method closer.
"Markus Villig was 19 when he declared war on Uber and Lyft.
Armed with zero experience and a tiny budget, he built Bolt into an $8.4B empire — from his small Estonian hometown.
His strategy was so brilliant it left Silicon Valley in awe.
Here's how he did it:"

Timeline Collapse: extreme low point → pivotal move → present contrast → delivery closer.
"The way Steve Jobs became a billionaire is insane:
Sold 99% of his Apple stock. Lost $50 million over 9 years. Then sold a side project to Disney for $7.4 BILLION.
Here's how the genius revival of a dying tech company made Jobs a billionaire before Apple did:"

## Thread Type 4 — Business/Strategy

**Dominant logic:** The strategy is the hero. The company is the vehicle for proving it worked.

**Distinct characteristics:**
- Business move must feel impossible or ruthless
- Competitor reaction always named and specific
- Financial numbers always exact — never approximated
- Strategy always replicable — reader should feel they can apply it

**What makes it credible:**
- Named competitors who lost or laughed
- Exact dollar figures tied to specific decisions
- The gap between what everyone predicted and what actually happened

**Pre-built structures:**

Ruthless Takeover: competitor laughed → catastrophic consequence for them → method closer.
"In 2016, this Indian billionaire launched a telecom company that gave everything away for FREE.
His competitors laughed.
6 months later, they lost $23 Billion.
Here's how Reliance Jio pulled off the most brutal market takeover in history:"

Hidden Strategy: secret weapon named then revealed → scale of psychological impact → closer.
"Costco's secret weapon isn't bulk discounts.
It's not their massive warehouses.
It's a psychological trap so powerful that 93% of US members renew every year.
Here's the brilliant strategy behind their $400 billion empire:"

## Thread Type 5 — Podcast/Interview Recap

**Dominant logic:** The conversation is the source. Writer's job is curation — not storytelling.

**Distinct characteristics:**
- Recency signal mandatory — "yesterday," "just," "last week"
- Speaker named immediately with credibility in one line
- Topics previewed in bullets inside the hook
- Writer's genuine reaction present — signals engagement not summary
- Closes with a number of insights plus a time-saving promise

**Pre-built structures:**

Bullet Preview: recency signal → speaker + credibility → bullet topics → numbered closer.
"Elon Musk's pod with Rogan yesterday is mind-blowing.
They discussed:
• The chances of Musk being assassinated
• Who's really protecting the Epstein files
• Why AI could end humanity by 2030
Let me save you 3 hours — here are the 12 most important things they talked about:"

Single Revelation: recency signal → speaker → one mind-blowing claim → implication closer.
"Yesterday, Sam Altman shared mind-blowing new insights on AI at Stanford.
I can't stop rewatching some of these clips.
6 key highlights you can't miss:"

## Thread Type 6 — Personal Authority

**Dominant logic:** The writer is the hero. Skin in the game is the credibility.

**Distinct characteristics:**
- Always opens with an "I" statement or credential
- Most vulnerable to voice mismatch — must sound genuinely human
- External authorities validate — not replace — writer's experience
- Closes with personal invitation or benefit-framed promise

**Pre-built structures:**

Belief Reversal: I used to think X → named authority changed it → what I found.
"I thought I was rational.
Then I read Daniel Kahneman's Nobel Prize-winning work on human decision-making.
He routinely asks 8 questions to expose cognitive traps you fall into daily.
Test yourself with these questions (it's the ultimate BS detector for your brain):"

Credential Pivot: professional credential + contrarian claim → reframe → numbered insights.
"As a therapist, I hate to break it to you, but...
If your nervous system is stuck in 'survival mode,' no amount of journaling or meditation will save you.
Here are 7 techniques that heal decades of trauma, increase your stress tolerance, and improve sleep/digestion..."

Pattern Observer: pattern noticed in specific group → one devastating example → implication.
"One pattern I've noticed in all miserable men:
They don't keep promises to themselves."

## How To Hold Future Thread Types

When a new thread type is identified, add it with:
- Dominant logic
- Distinct characteristics
- What makes it credible
- Pre-built structures with canonical examples (minimum 2-3)
