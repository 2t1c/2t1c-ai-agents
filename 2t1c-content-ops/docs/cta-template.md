## The 5-Part Close (single continuous post format)

Every Long-Form Post ends with this exact structure, inline at the end of the same post (no reply tweet):

```
1. ENGAGEMENT QUESTION (write fresh, 1 sentence)
2. BRIDGE with reciprocity gift (write fresh, 1-2 sentences)
3. PRODUCT CTA (exact wording — never change)
4. BRAND CTA (exact wording — never change)
5. ATTRIBUTION (write fresh, 1 line)
```

Total CTA budget: ~400-450 chars. Plus body ~1100 chars = post total ~1300-1450 chars.

---

## Part 1 — Engagement Question

One question that invites replies. Must be:

- **Low-friction** — anyone can answer
- **Personal** — about the reader's situation
- **Directly relevant** to the post's topic

**Examples:**

| Post topic | Question |
|---|---|
| Institutional defense (Semmelweis-style) | *"Where in your own life is the uncomfortable truth being defended by the people who should want it most?"* |
| Paradigm blindness (AlexNet-style) | *"Where in your own field is the 'dead end' paradigm quietly rebuilding?"* |
| System redefinition (Nixon Shock-style) | *"Where in your life is a 'temporary' story quietly becoming permanent?"* |
| Architectural authority (Milgram-style) | *"What room are you sitting in that makes the wrong thing feel required?"* |

**Avoid:**
- Generic "What do you think?" — no friction-lowering, no specificity
- Yes/no questions that close the loop instead of opening conversation
- Questions about the historical figure ("What was Semmelweis really like?") — these are about the post, not the reader

## Part 2 — Bridge with Reciprocity Pre-Frame

The bridge is **a gift**. Extract a transferable rule from the post's specific mechanism and hand it to the reader as a tool they can use this week, **independent of whether they click the product link.**

### Structure

1. Name the transferable pattern the post just demonstrated
2. Give a usable instruction — something the reader can apply before the end of the week
3. The product pivot then feels like "and here's more of the same," not "and here's the pitch"

### Examples

**After a Semmelweis-style institutional-defense post:**
> One pattern worth keeping: when an outcome is measured by the people being measured, the measurement is protecting them, not reporting on them. Run this lens on any institutional metric that has survived 20 years of critique.

**After an AlexNet-style paradigm-blindness post:**
> One pattern worth keeping: when every expert in a field agrees something is impossible, that's usually where the next breakthrough is forming. Watch for the idea that was once alive, got killed by a prestigious rival, and has been sitting dormant for 20-30 years.

**After a Nixon Shock-style system-rewrite post:**
> One lens worth keeping: when any institution calls something "temporary," multiply the duration by 200. Nixon's 90-day gold suspension has lasted 54 years.

**After a Milgram-style architecture-of-obedience post:**
> When a system demands something that feels off, the architecture is doing the work. Change the room before you trust your own conviction.

### What makes these work

- They give the reader something **usable before Friday.** That's the reciprocity mechanism firing.
- The product pivot stops being a pivot. It becomes "I just showed you one; I have 100 more."
- The bridge alone, if screenshotted, is a standalone useful post. **That's the signal you've crossed the reciprocity threshold.**

### What to avoid

- Abstract bridges ("mental models help you see things early") — no gift, just a claim
- Generic instruction ("think before you invest") — the reader already does that
- Product-shaped bridges ("this is what the toolkit teaches") — reads as pitch, not gift
- More than one transferable rule per bridge — dilutes the takeaway

## Part 3 — Product CTA (exact wording — never change)

```
I made a free toolkit breaking down 100+ mental models used by history's greatest thinkers.

5,000+ downloads. 113 five-star reviews.

Grab your free copy here: https://besuperhuman.gumroad.com/l/mentalmodels
```

### Rules

- **Direct Gumroad link.** Embed the URL inline so readers can grab the product immediately with no friction.
- **No "Comment MODELS"** or any comment keyword anywhere in the post. The keyword mechanic is deprecated.
- **Never say "link in bio."**
- **Don't paraphrase.** Use this wording verbatim.

## Part 4 — Brand CTA (exact wording — never change)

```
If you're new here, @GeniusGTX is a gallery for the greatest minds in economics, psychology, and history. Follow along for more similar content.
```

### Rules

- Verbatim. Do not vary phrasing.
- Always immediately after Product CTA.
- Always before Attribution.

## Part 5 — Attribution (write fresh)

### Format

```
— [Source Name], [Platform] | Data: [institutions or studies cited]
```

### Examples

| Source | Format |
|---|---|
| Lex Fridman podcast with Ray Dalio | `— Lex Fridman Podcast, YouTube \| Data: Federal Reserve, BLS` |
| Sherwin Nuland book on Semmelweis | `— Sherwin B. Nuland, *The Doctors' Plague* \| Data: Vienna General Hospital records` |
| Krizhevsky/Sutskever/Hinton paper | `— Krizhevsky, Sutskever, Hinton, *ImageNet Classification with Deep CNNs* (2012) \| Data: ImageNet LSVRC` |
| William Greider book on the Fed | `— William Greider, *Secrets of the Temple* \| Data: Federal Reserve, US Treasury` |
| Stanley Milgram's published research | `— Stanley Milgram, *Obedience to Authority* (1974) \| Data: Yale University experiments` |

### Rules

- **No view counts.** Credit the original source and data providers, not engagement metrics.
- Always the **final line** of the post.
- Include the year when the source is a book or paper.
- "Data:" field surfaces the underlying authority — specific institutions or studies, not "research" or "studies show."

## Tuki QRT Variations

For Tuki QRT format, the close can be tighter. The bridge follows lowercase casual style. Parts 3 and 4 stay verbatim.

```
[lowercase reflection or transferable rule, 1-2 sentences]

I made a free toolkit breaking down 100+ mental models used by history's greatest thinkers.

5,000+ downloads. 113 five-star reviews.

Grab your free copy here: https://besuperhuman.gumroad.com/l/mentalmodels

If you're new here, @GeniusGTX is a gallery for the greatest minds in economics, psychology, and history. Follow along for more similar content.

— [Source attribution]
```

The engagement question is optional in Tuki QRT — the QRTed tweet often carries the conversation prompt naturally.

## Anti-Patterns

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---|---|
| "Comment MODELS for the toolkit" | Direct gumroad link inline |
| "Link in bio" | Direct gumroad link inline |
| "That's a wrap." | Removed — never use |
| "Follow me for more content like this" | Use the verbatim Brand CTA |
| Generic question ("What do you think?") | Specific question rooted in this post's mechanism |
| Generic bridge ("mental models help you see early") | Reciprocity gift — a usable lens the reader can apply this week |
| "— Source: [post URL]" or "— @gothburz on X" with view counts | "— [Author], *[Title]* \| Data: [institutions]" |
